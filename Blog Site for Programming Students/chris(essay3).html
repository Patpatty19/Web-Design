Becoming a CIITeen: What Track and/or Strand to Pursue?

Christian I. Denzon
April 25, 2021

When I was in Junior Highschool I often wondered what Track and Strand I wanted to pursue because I was unsure on what I wanted to really be back then whether it was being a doctor, programmer, or etc. In the end, I chose the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood track and then chose the ICT strand to learn programming. There are three tracks to choose from when you first enter Senior High School, the three tracks are the Academic, Technical-Vocational-Livelihood, and Sports and arts. In the Academic Track there are three strands Accountancy Business Management (ABM), General Academic Strand (GAS), Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS), and Science Technology Engineering and Mathematics (STEM). In the TVL Track there are four strands Information and Communications Technology (ICT), Home Economics, Agri-Fishery Arts, and Industrial Arts. In the sports track, there are not really any specific strands but if you are into sports like playing, or coaching, then this is the right track for you to take. In CIIT, there are currently 2 tracks available to students, those tracks are Arts and Design Track - Media and Visual Arts Strand, and Tech Voc Track – ICT Strand. The basis for the comparison is what job/career paths these tracks give to students and their specializations. I personally believe that students need the chance to explore different tracks so that they can find what they really want to pursue.

First, let us compare Computer Programming and Animation which are both from the TVL track and ICT strand. The differences of these two are, in Computer Programming they are more focused on software development, programming/coding, game development, and etc. Computer Programming specializes in learning different kinds of programming languages and develop working programs. Animation on the other hand focuses more on Traditional Animation, Digital Animation, 2D Animation, 3D Modeling, and etc. Animation specializes in drawing and developing models in 2D or 3D for animating scenes. The similarities of these two strands are that they both need computers to do work on and that certain skill and knowledge is needed to actually use the tools needed for starting Programming and Animation. These two can also work together to create stunning 3D model animations for video games, films, and more.

Second, let us compare Animation to Media and Visual Arts. The differences of these two are, in Animation, as I’ve mentioned before, it is more focused on drawing and developing models, however, in Media and Visual Arts, it is more focused in the media and creative industry, more on illustrations, graphic design, and digital art, and it includes career options like Videographer, Photographer, Production Designer, Graphic Designer, Illustrator, and Digital Artist. The similarity of these two is that they both participate in drawing to produce visual outputs. These two can work together to create animated art for professional use like vector art.

Last of all, I would like to compare Media and Visual Arts to Computer Programming. I have already mentioned before the differences of each of these strands, as for their similarities, they can collaborate together to create professional looking websites with stunning visual art, friendly user interfaces, and website layouts.

In conclusion, I still believe that students need the opportunity to truly explore the Tracks and Strands that are available for their school. All of the strands that are offered in CIIT have the capability to cooperate which each other, for example, if Animation and Computer Programming collaborated with each other, the skills that they have learned will be used to create 3D model animations, visual effects, and etc. I advise future incoming CIITeens to research on what they truly want to do because CIIT gives their all to make way for the dreams of students, especially if the students really pursue what they like to do.
