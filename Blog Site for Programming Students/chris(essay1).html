The Pythagorean Model

Christian I. Denzon
March 24, 2021

I’m sure we’ve always wondered on how the stars and space looked like when we were a child. Now that we’ve all gotten older, we get more curious because of the complexity of what’s out there. However, we must look back on how the ancient people looked at our universe before we came to our current theories about space. One of these theories is the Pythagorean Model, which is proposed by Philolaus, a student of Pythagoras. You may also know Pythagoras from the Pythagorean Theorem. Pythagoras’ theory about the model of the universe helped shape on what our current theories have become because of his teachings that inspired his students.

Pythagoras was known to be the first one that assumed the Earth is round, and that the heavenly bodies move around in circles. In the model that is proposed by him, the Earth is at rest at the center of the universe while everything rotates around it. He believed that the other planets and stars revolved around the center which in his model is the Earth. Pythagoras also said that the motions of the planets were mathematically related to musical sounds and numbers, and he called these “The Music of the Spheres”. After the declaration of his theories, his students or apprentices followed him in his footsteps in theorizing. One of his students is called Anaxagoras, he was credited with having figured out the relative positions of the sun, the moon, and Earth during solar and lunar eclipses. Another student of Pythagoras is Philolaus, he is the one that proposed that the Earth and the other planets did not revolve around the sun but rather a “central fire”.

Although there are many other theories about the universe, I believe that because of Pythagoras and his philosophies and teachings, he has inspired many others to go in his footsteps to theorize on the universe. And because of that, our theories about are world or solar system today has been figured out. In conclusion, the theories that has been proposed by Pythagoras and then adopted by his students like Anaxagoras and Philolaus, has helped shaped our theories today.
