How to Do CPR in a Correct and Safe Way

Patrick Miguel M. Babala
April 19, 2021

Saving one’s life makes you awesome and at the same time a savior. Saving one’s life is not easy and will never be easy. A chance of ending one’s life can be done if you do It in a wrong order or step. CPR or Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation is a technique used by many people when it comes to emergencies, life ending situation. This technique can be learned by any normal person. Learning the technique will be useful when you are in a severe situation wherein someone is dying and his/her life is in danger. You can save someone if they suddenly had a heart attack or drowning resulting their heartbeat to stop (Barell, A. 2020). Steps on doing CPR is hard because you are in charge of the life of the someone who is dying and one wrong move could end the life of the one you are trying to save. Here are the steps on how to do CPR in a correct and safe way to prevent any major casualties to the victim.

The first step in performing CPR is to prepare yourself first. Put your mindset wherein you are trying to save somebody and saving him/her is the biggest priority at that moment. Prepare yourself for the worst. Keep calm and do not let anxiety and nervousness eat you up. Check the scene and the person. If he/she is not responding, call 911 or ask someone to call 911. Try to find a professional in the scene and seek for their help. Try to hold out as much as the victim can to wait for the professionals to arrive in the scene.

Next, place the person carefully on their back in a safe spot and kneel beside their chest. Be extremely cautious on the person that you are trying to save. Remember, he/she is not conscious at the moment. Tilt their head back slightly by lifting their chin. Check inside their mouth if he or she is choking. Remove any object or food that could be blocking the airway. Be extremely careful when you are trying to get the object because it may result to fully be pushed farther.

The next step is to check the breathing of the victim. Place your ear next to the person’s mouth and listen for 10 seconds. If he/she is not breathing or not gasping, begin performing CPR. If the victim is still breathing do not perform CPR. Only monitor their breathing and perform CPR if the victim stopped breathing. Do not perform straight CPR because instead of saving the victim, chances of being more damaged can happen.

The fourth step is to perform chest compressions. There are different kinds of positions of hand and fingers depending on the victim. This differs when it comes to adults, children, and infants. Doing the adult CPR requires both hands on top of the other. With the heel of the hands and straight elbows, push hard and fast in the center of the chest, slightly below the nipples. Perform 30 chest compressions at a rate of 100 per minute. Be extremely careful when it comes to children especially infants.

The next step is the most important step and could save someone’s life. At this stage, rescue breaths are performed. After removing an object that could be blocking the airway, you have the go signal to do rescue breaths. Pinch their nose shut, place your mouth fully over theirs and blow to make their chest rise. If their chest does not rise with the first breath, retilt their head. If it still doesn’t rise, the person might be choking. Check if something is still blocking the airway and immediately remove it carefully. One wrong move and it could be pushed farther away.

The last step is to repeat all the steps you performed. Do all these steps in order until the person becomes conscious. Do this technique when you are still waiting for the professionals to arrive in the scene. Hold out as much as you can until ambulance arrives. If an AED or Automated External Defibrillator arrives, carry on performing CPR until the machine is set up and ready to use.

Doing CPR is not a joke and extremely hard to do. With the pressure that comes from literally saving one’s life is so hard to bear. CPR usually performs when someone is not breathing. You only have 8 minutes to spare if you are not breathing. More than 8 minutes, death arrives. Be sure to check if the victim is not responding to any calls before doing CPR. CPR should be taught to everyone because anything could happen in anytime. Practicing chest compressions and rescue breaths can help someone to survive. Always remember, only use CPR when the victim has stopped breathing. Hold out as much as you can until help arrives. If the victim is conscious, calm the victim down and support his/her body and help them to move in to a safer place and position. Talk to the victim but do not bombard them with words that could potentially increase stress. Saving the life of a certain someone is should always be taken seriously. Learning this technique won’t hurt anybody and could potentially save someone at any given time. Being a savior of one’s life doesn’t sound bad. You could also save someone from a traumatic feeling and experience that will last until their day of death.
