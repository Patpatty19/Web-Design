5 Types of Online Students: What type of student are you?

Christian I. Denzon
April 25, 2021

In this time of the pandemic, online learning is the only choice that students can do to still attend school, however, students may find online learning difficult, a hassle, challenging, or some might say that it makes them inspired, and engaging. Because of those reasons, students may skip class, sleep during class, or some may attend and participate actively. The Online Students/Learners is an umbrella term that includes a variety of types of students that attend school online. If you’re a student that is attending online school, it is inevitable that you will fall under one of the five student types.

The first type that I will be talking about is the type of student that always sleep in class and does not participate, in our flowchart that student is called the “ZZZZZZ Student/Ghost Student”. To describe the Ghost Student is very simple, a Ghost Student is typically a student that does not attend class at all or a student that just sleep in class therefore it also being called the “ZZZZZZ Student”. This idea is supported because of people I know that sleep in class a lot or attend class but don’t participate.

The second type of student that I will talking about next is the student that is always on Social Media. The Social Media student can be identified if they attend class but is on other tabs or apps and if they use social media while in class and if they don’t participate. That series of conditions leads to a student being identified as a Social Media Student. This is supported by the fact that all students have Social Media and since they aren’t actively monitored like the camera isn’t turned on, they can just access their devices anytime and the teacher would not notice.

The third type of student is the Gamer Student. The Gamer Student can be identified by the following conditions: The gamer student still attends class, they use other apps while in class, and if they play games while in class. Gamer students tend to still attend in class but once attended, they will minimize the class and focus back on their games, not paying attention to the lecture that is ongoing. This is supported by a very recent viral video that went into trending where the student was playing while in class and he didn’t mute his mic leading to him being heard by his teacher and his fellow classmates. They tried to call for him but after some analyzation, it became obvious that the student had his class muted so he couldn’t hear anything that was being said in the class.

The fourth type of student is the Connection Problem Student. This type of student can be easily identified if they often have bad internet connection and if they keep disconnecting in class. In my opinion, these types of students are very common in the Philippines. This is supported by the fact that we don’t have good affordable internet in the Philippines, because of that, people have no choice but to settle with the internet that they have which are usually slow.

The fifth and last type of student that I will be talking about is the Active Student. The Active Student is usually the type of student that is always attending class, and the one that always asks the teacher with questions about the lecture. These students usually chat with teachers a lot and they always answer attentively. These are supported by the fact that there is always that one or few students that actively participate in class which makes them stand out from the others.

As long as you’re a student attending online school, you will be in no doubt be classified into at the least one type of online student out of the five available types. The five types of student are: The Ghost Student, the one who sleeps in class and doesn’t participate, The Social Media Student, the one who is all over social media all day, the Gamer Student, the one who attends but just sets aside the class and continue to play, the Connection Problem Student, the one who has bad internet, and lastly the Active Student, the role model of every class and the one who always participates on what’s going on. In conclusion, I believe that every online student falls into one of the types/category and I also believe that their type can change from time to time.
