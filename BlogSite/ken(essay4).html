5 Types of Online Students: What type of student are you?

Ken Adrian A. Alupit
April 16, 2021

We live in a pandemic time right now. Almost all of the activities we do before are now done on the internet, and this includes education. The sudden shift in the delivery of knowledge reveals new types of students in the online environment. Students are still students even if the place and mode of delivery have changed, but there will surely be changes. Being in an online environment, it is easier for students to be themselves behind the camera. Compared to the face-to-face approach of the later years, the behavior between students varies from one to the other. Students now show behaviors that they cannot do when in a face-to-face environment.

The most common type of student that can be seen during online classes is the ghost students. The ghost students are students who attend online classes but don't participate or cooperate with teachers and their classmates. They sometimes even do not attend classes at all. The punctuality between ghost students varies from one to the other. Some do all their work and pass them on time, while others do not. Some of the ghost students interact with their classmates, but when it comes to online classes with the teachers, the ghost student will disappear. There are also ghost students where they interact with teachers while in online class, but do not interact with their classmates after online classes like in group works or group projects. The ghost students' reasons as to why they do what they do vary, but one of the significant reasons is sleeping, and some of the other reasons are partly connected to the other types of students.

Some students browse social media apps and sites while in an online class. These are the social media students. Sharing posts, memes, and talking to other people are some of the examples that social media students do while in an online class. Most of the social media students don't listen to the teachers, who are conducting the online class, and they don't participate as well. Some can manage to be attentive and participate in the online class while they do their stuff, but this is rare. You can usually see social media students share posts or memes on social media while the online class is still ongoing. Most of the social media students cooperate and interact with their classmates when the class is over. Social media students' reason for not being attentive in an online class and resorting to browsing social media could be because they find themselves bored and uninterested in the class. This can be also a way for them to cope with stress. This type of student could also fall into the ghost students if they are unresponsive or uncooperative with their classmates and teachers.

One of the most common types of students that can be seen in online classes is gamer students. Gamer students usually play online games or games in general during online classes. Most of these students don't listen or participate in the online class because they are too preoccupied with playing. You can usually see gamer students' status in Discord if they are playing something. They don't stop, even if there is an upcoming online class, but they will join online classes when it starts for the sake of attendance and nothing else. Because of this, they sometimes forget to leave the online class. Gamer students also cannot be bothered with group activities or group projects, especially with meetings. This student is another type of student that is partly related to the ghost students.

Another one of the most common types of students in the online class environment is the connection problem students. These students are the ones that have the most problems when it comes to the internet. Slow connection speed, internet interruptions, laggy and delayed audio, video signals, and more are the common problems when it comes to the students' internet. This results in the student being late for an online class or being completely absent, incapable of participating in class, inability to speak, and many more. Because of the sudden switch to an online environment for education. A lot of students who don't have a good and stable internet connection are now struggling. The connection problem students is another type of student that is partly related to the ghost students.

These are the students who participate and cooperate in online classes and even before when it was a face-to-face environment. They are called active students. Usually, they belong to the group of students who excel in class. In an online class environment, the active students are the ones who generally talk and socialize with the teachers, and they are the ones who frequently recite in class. They are the ones who fill in the gap for the students, who don't participate in online classes like the ones mentioned before. Some of the active students in online classes are not active before when it was a face-to-face environment. They had only been more active when classes switched to an online environment because some students are more comfortable working at home and by themselves. Active students actively participate in school activities and group projects and they also pass all of their requirements in time.

The five types of students seen in an online class are ghost students, social media students, gamer students, internet problem students, and active students. Because of the shift to online education, students now show behaviors that they cannot reveal when classes were still in a face-to-face environment. It is easier for students to do whatever they want while in an online class because there is no supervision from the teachers. Although students can do everything they want, there are still problems that face them hindering their education. Mental health problems and internet problems are two of the most common ones.

Because of the sudden changes in the delivery of education due to the pandemic, we can also see changes in the students. Online education is not the best for the students and teachers right now, but these kinds of things are what builds a better future. Hopefully, we can learn from our mistakes today and build a better future for education
